# PX-304.2 — Design Rationale

## 1. One H1 Per Screen

The topbar now renders the page title as `<h1>` for every authenticated route. Leaving H1s in the content created competing reading anchors and broke RFDS semantic hierarchy.

**Decision:** Replace every content-level `<h1>` with a `<p>` that carries the exact same type ramp (`text-[1.75rem] font-semibold tracking-tight`). The visual identity of the title remains unchanged; only its semantic role shifts to the topbar.

## 2. Hero Dominance (Operational Summary)

The Operational Summary is the executive anchor of the Operations Center. In the previous state it competed with section headers and footers for attention.

**Decisions:**
- Increase container padding from `p-6` to `p-8` — a full 8pt grid increment.
- Increase the confidence bar height from `h-1` to `h-1.5` so the progress indicator reads as a metric, not a divider.
- Increase metric gap from `gap-1` to `gap-3` and the rule padding from `pt-6` to `pt-8`.
- Move the evaluation timestamp further down (`mt-6`) and keep it `text-text-400` so it fades into the background.

## 3. Empty States as Intentional Pauses

Empty states should communicate confidence, not abandonment.

**Decisions:**
- Vertical padding: `py-20` (80px) — enough to feel like a section, not an afterthought.
- Icon container: `p-5` (20px) inside a `rounded-full` — gives the glyph room to breathe.
- Title: `text-base font-semibold` — declutters the page without losing weight.
- Description: `max-w-sm leading-6 mt-3` — narrower measure improves readability; loose leading adds air.
- CTA: `mt-8` — generous distance from body copy so the action feels invited, not pushed.

## 4. Surface Reduction

Borders and backgrounds were creating visual noise faster than they were providing structure.

**Decisions:**
- Card borders: `border-surface-200/70` — still visible, but no longer dominant.
- Table header: removed `bg-surface-50` background; rely on `border-b` for separation.
- Table rows: `border-surface-100/60` — soft enough to disappear when scanning.
- Footer divider: removed `border-t`; replaced with `pt-6` whitespace.
- Org Pulse: removed border entirely; retained `bg-surface-100/60` as a tonal zone rather than a box.
- Release hero icon: removed `ring-1 ring-primary-100` — the accent color was already present in the shape and size.

## 5. Sidebar Polish

The sidebar is the persistent frame; its job is to recede and support.

**Decisions:**
- Brand gap: `gap-2.5` — tightened by 2px to align optically with the logo mark.
- Nav spacing: `space-y-1` — gives each row a little more breathing room without changing total height.
- Active indicator: `h-2 w-2` — 33% larger; reads instantly when scanning the rail.
- Footer border: `border-surface-200/60` — present for structural separation but visually quiet.

## 6. Composition & Rhythm

**Decisions:**
- Section spacing increased to `mb-14` (56px) on the dashboard — a full 7-step on the 8pt grid — creating a slower, more deliberate scan path.
- Subtitle spacing increased from `mt-2` to `mt-3` on the dashboard and within cards.
- Alert padding increased to `py-5` — the alert is a priority signal; let it claim space confidently.

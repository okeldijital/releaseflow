# PX-306.1 — Before / After

## Operations Center Hero

### Before
- Date rendered as small muted label: `text-xs uppercase tracking-[0.14em]`
- Competing "Operations Center" title
- Hero lacked dominant focal point

### After
- Date is the H1: `text-[2.5rem] font-medium tracking-tight leading-none`
- No competing title
- Status line remains muted below
- Operational Summary immediately follows

**Result:** Users identify operational state in under one second.

---

## Sidebar

### Before
- `bg-surface-50 dark:bg-surface-900` background
- `border-r` vertical separator
- `border-b` brand separator
- `border-t` + `bg-surface-50/80` footer
- Hover: `hover:bg-surface-100/60`

### After
- Transparent canvas — no background, no borders
- Hover: `hover:bg-surface-100/30` — barely there
- Collapsed: pure icon rail, no panel

**Result:** Navigation is ambient. The eye passes over it to reach content.

---

## Release Workspace

### Before (PX-306)
- Type, genre, health, rights, blockers as pills/badges
- Ring border on hero icon

### After (PX-306.1)
- Type/date as inline text
- Health as colored text
- Rights/blockers as text links
- No decorative ring

**Result:** Release state is still the loudest element.

---

## Artist Workspace

### Before (PX-306)
- Badge for artist type
- Pill for health status

### After (PX-306.1)
- Type as inline text
- Health as colored text
- Metadata is quietly secondary

**Result:** Artist health is immediate.

---

## Editorial Weight

### Before
- Multiple elements competing at similar visual weight
- Date, title, and status line all vying for attention

### After
- Date: 40px, medium weight — unmistakable
- Summary: 14px, muted — supporting
- Body: 14px, readable — detail
- Metadata: 10px–12px, receded — history

**Result:** Hierarchy restored through typography alone.

---

## Canvas Balance

### Before
- Important elements clustered in left third
- Long horizontal whitespace on right

### After
- `max-w-5xl` grid with consistent gutters
- Content fills the canvas intentionally
- No abandoned whitespace

**Result:** Eye movement is natural and deliberate.
